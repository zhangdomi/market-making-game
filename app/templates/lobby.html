Welcome to the Market making game!

Objective

Maximize your expected profit over a series of trading rounds.

How to Play

At the beginning of each round, you will be presented with a set of cards. These cards are valued between 2 and 14, with the Ace being worth 14. Some of these cards may be turned face up, while others remain face down.

Market Maker Quote

Alongside the cards, you'll see a market maker quote displayed as X at Y. Here's what they represent:

  • X (Bid): The price the market maker is willing to buy at.
  • Y (Ask): The price the market maker is willing to sell at.

Time to trade

  • Decide the number of units you wish to trade.
  • Choose whether you want to buy (at the Ask price) or sell (at the Bid price) those units at the given market maker quote
  • Just like in the real market, an offer remains valid only for a limited amount of time. So, act quickly!

Valid orders

  • You should be able to afford any long position you take as well as cover the maximum loss for any short position based on your budget.
  • Failure to do so will result in a 50$ deduction.

Outcome

Once you make a decision (buy, sell or skip) the cards are turned over:

  • The sum of the card values represent the market price for that round.
  • Based on your decision and the resulting market price, you'll either realize a profit or a loss.

Track your position

After each round, you'll be asked to calculate your profit or losses.

Good luck!
